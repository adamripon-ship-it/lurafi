## Brand Overview

Lurafi sells Kevin, a Swiss-engineered AI presence simulation device for home security. Kevin is designed to make a home look and sound occupied when residents are away, so potential burglars are deterred before attempting a break-in.

The brand positioning is prevention, not reaction. Traditional alarms often respond after entry. Cameras can record evidence, but they do not necessarily stop a burglary in the moment. Kevin focuses on making the home appear lived-in from outside.

## Privacy and Data

Kevin has no cameras and no microphones. It does not monitor the home with video or audio. Simulations are stored locally on the device, not streamed from the cloud or shared with third parties.

This privacy model is a major differentiator for customers who want deterrence without surveillance.

## How Kevin Works

1. Place Kevin near a window or visible area.
2. Connect it with one power cable.
3. Configure routines in the Kevin app.
4. Kevin simulates believable presence using light, shadow, and sound.
5. From outside, the home appears occupied, reducing the chance that burglars select it as a target.
