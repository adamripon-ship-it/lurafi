## Brand Overview

Mitipi sells Kevin®, a Swiss-engineered and designed AI presence simulation device for home security. Kevin is built to make a home look and sound occupied when residents are away, so potential burglars are deterred before attempting a break-in.

Brand line: Smart. Secure. Swiss.

The positioning is prevention, not reaction. Alarms often respond after entry. Cameras can record evidence but may fail when Wi-Fi is jammed. Kevin focuses on believable occupancy from outside—before anyone tests the door.

## Product

Product name: Kevin®

Category:
- AI home security
- Burglary deterrent / innovative burglary prevention
- Presence simulation
- Smart home security device
- Privacy-first home protection

Kevin uses light scenarios, visual shadow effects, and everyday sounds (cleaning, cooking, conversations, playing) to simulate realistic household activity. No cameras or microphones.

Use cases: homes, apartments, offices, shops, warehouses, holiday and second homes, multiple properties via one app.

## Core Benefits

- Deter burglaries before they happen
- Makes an empty home look and sound occupied from outside
- Works without cameras or microphones (100% privacy)
- Stores simulations locally on the device
- Jammer-resilient: core protection continues when Wi-Fi security is disabled
- Plug and play — typical use around 9W
- 70+ hours of built-in simulations plus upload your own sounds
- AI-generated household sounds adapt over time
- App: schedules, geo-fencing, instant activation, multi-device
- Bluetooth speaker and lamp when you are at home
- Complements existing alarm systems

## Privacy and Data

Kevin has no cameras and no microphones. It does not monitor the home with video or audio. Simulations are stored locally on the device, not streamed from the cloud or shared with third parties.

## How Kevin Works

1. Place Kevin near a window or visible area with a wall or ceiling for shadows.
2. Connect with one power cable (approx. 3 m).
3. Configure routines, schedules, or geo-fencing in the Kevin app.
4. Kevin simulates believable presence using light, shadow, and sound from local storage.
5. From outside, the home appears occupied—reducing the chance burglars select it as a target.
